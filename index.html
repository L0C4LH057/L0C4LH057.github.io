Root for Your Friends

13 May 2025 personal

In the security community, we often focus intensely on technical skills, certifications, and individual achievements. While these are important, there's another aspect that's equally vital but often overlooked: actively supporting and celebrating the success of others.

When a colleague lands a great job, gets a CVE published, or releases a new tool, our instinct might be to compare their success to our own progress. Instead, I encourage you to practice genuine enthusiasm for their accomplishments.

This mindset shift does several important things:

First, it creates a positive community culture. Security work can be stressful and isolating. Celebrating others' wins builds camaraderie and support networks that sustain us through challenging projects.

Second, it combats imposter syndrome. When we're happy for others' success, we internalize that achievement is possible and recognize that multiple people can succeed simultaneously.

Third, it opens opportunities. People remember who supported them and are more likely to collaborate with or recommend those who genuinely championed their work.

Practical ways to implement this:

  • Share colleagues' work on social media with meaningful commentary
  • Recommend people for opportunities even when you're not asked
  • Send a congratulatory message for milestones and achievements
  • Publicly acknowledge when someone helps you solve a problem

This isn't about false praise - it's about recognizing that another person's success doesn't diminish your own. In fact, a rising tide lifts all boats. When we celebrate each other, we strengthen the entire security ecosystem.
